# Platform Content Strategy
### AI Ad Creative Playbook — Facebook, Instagram, TikTok

> **Who this is for:** AI agents generating ad creative for product clients.
> **First client reference:** Salvia Lion — handmade Reiki-charged bath salts for empaths.
> **Core principle:** Lead with value. Make people feel something or learn something first. The product is the natural next step, not the pitch.

## Facebook

### Image Ads (Feed)

**When to use:** Retargeting, direct response, conversions. Image outperforms video on CPA for bottom-funnel.

**What works:**
- Single product, clean background, strong contrast
- Minimal or no text in the image itself (let copy do the work)
- 4:5 vertical (1080×1350px) takes more scroll real estate than square
- UGC-style product photos outperform studio shots — a hand holding the product in natural light beats a white-background render

**Copy formula:**

```
Headline (5–8 words): Hit the pain or the transformation
Body (2–4 sentences):
  Line 1 — Name the problem/feeling they already have
  Line 2 — Introduce the shift (not the product, the feeling after)
  Line 3 — The product as the vehicle
  Line 4 — Social proof or specificity ("1,200+ reviews" / "ships in 2 days")
CTA: Shop Now / Try It / Get Yours
```

**Example structure for Salvia Lion:**
> *Headline:* For when your nervous system needs a reset.
> *Body:* You absorb everything — other people's moods, the noise, the weight of the day. These bath salts were made for that. Reiki-charged, mineral-rich, handmade in small batches. 4.9 stars from 800+ customers.
> *CTA:* Shop Salvia Lion

**Visual approach:** Warm amber or green tones. Natural light. Product in hand or in bath water. No text overlays. Feels like a friend's Instagram, not an ad.

### Video Ads (Facebook Feed + Stories)

**When to use:** Awareness, top-of-funnel cold audiences, retargeting with social proof.

**Length:** 15–30 seconds for feed. 6–15 seconds for Stories.

**Structure:**

| Second | What happens |
|---|---|
| 0–3 | Hook — motion, surprising visual, or bold text. No logo. |
| 4–10 | Problem or relatable feeling (no product yet) |
| 11–20 | Product reveal as solution — demo or transformation |
| 21–30 | Social proof + CTA |

**What works:**
- Designed to work without sound (captions on, key points as text overlay)
- UGC-style wins over polished production — handheld, natural light, real person
- First frame must stop the scroll — use motion, bold contrast, or unexpected visual
- Avoid hard cuts in the first 2 seconds; use slow reveal or zoom

**Hook formats that convert:**
- Question: "Do you hold onto energy that isn't yours?"
- Statement: "This is what my Saturday night looks like now."
- Visual pattern interrupt: Close-up of salts pouring into steaming water, slow motion

**CTA placement:** On-screen text at second 20+ and spoken. "Link in bio" does not work in ads — use "Shop Now" button.

## Instagram

### Feed Image Ads

**When to use:** Discovery, brand building, soft conversion.

**Format:** 4:5 vertical (1080×1350px) — takes 78% more screen space than square.

**What works:**
- Lifestyle imagery over product-only shots (product in context of a ritual, a mood, a moment)
- One dominant focal point — do not crowd the frame
- Earthy, muted palettes for wellness/spiritual products; high contrast for fashion/DTC
- Faces outperform products alone — even a hand or body part creates human connection
- Minimal text in the image

**Copy formula (IG feed skews shorter than FB):**

```
First line (hook — visible before "more"):
  Either a question, bold claim, or single emotional statement
Body (3–5 lines max):
  Feeling → shift → product → proof
Hashtags: 3–5 max, niche-specific
CTA: in the last line of copy, not just the button
```

**Visual approach for Salvia Lion:** Bath water with petals floating, candlelight, steam. Warm tones. Feels like self-care content you'd bookmark — not an ad you'd scroll past.

### Instagram Reels Ads

**When to use:** Cold audience discovery, top-of-funnel, reach. Reels deliver 26% lower CPC than feed.

**Length:** 15–30 seconds is the sweet spot. Under 60s max.

**Format:** 9:16 vertical, 1080×1920px. Keep key visuals out of the bottom 35% (UI overlays).

**Structure:**

| Second | What happens |
|---|---|
| 0–3 | Hook — native-style, feels organic, NOT an opening logo |
| 4–10 | Education, storytelling, or emotional validation (still no hard sell) |
| 11–20 | Product reveal embedded naturally in the story |
| 21–30 | Soft CTA — "this is where I got mine" style, then Shop button |

**What works:**
- Sound-on by default — use music, voiceover, or both. Sound-on Reels outperform silent by 35%.
- Looks like organic creator content — lo-fi, handheld, jump cuts, text overlays
- On-screen text reinforces the audio (accessibility + muted scrollers)
- Creator POV or testimonial format consistently outperforms brand POV

**Hook formats that convert on Reels:**
- "I didn't expect a bath to change my week but here we are."
- "Things I do when I need to protect my energy:"
- "POV: you finally found something that actually helps."

**Avoid:** Brand intro in first 3 seconds. Polished 4K footage that screams "advertisement." Logos before second 5.

---

### Instagram Stories Ads

**When to use:** Retargeting, impulse, limited offers. Strong swipe-up (link) behavior.

**Length:** 6–15 seconds. Users are already swiping.

**Format:** 9:16 vertical. Keep all text and product in the middle 60% of the frame.

**What works:**
- Single message only — one idea, one CTA
- Bold contrast text on a clean background
- Countdown sticker for urgency (if organic stories; approximated in ad design)
- "Swipe Up" or on-screen CTA arrow visual even when using button

## TikTok

### In-Feed Video (Cold Audiences)

**When to use:** Discovery, new audience reach, top-of-funnel. Best for wellness, lifestyle, emotional products.

**Length:** 21–34 seconds is TikTok's own reported sweet spot. 15–30s is the practical floor/ceiling.

**Format:** 9:16, 1080×1920px. Safe zone for text: top 10% and bottom 20% are UI territory.

**Structure:**

| Second | What happens |
|---|---|
| 0–3 | Hook — creates tension, curiosity, or instant recognition |
| 4–15 | Educational or emotional body — teach, validate, show transformation |
| 16–25 | Product reveal as a natural conclusion to the story |
| 26–34 | CTA — spoken + on-screen text |

**Hook formats that work on TikTok:**
- Relatable problem statement: "Nobody talks about how exhausting it is to feel everything."
- Surprising claim: "I stopped dreading Sundays when I started doing this."
- List format: "5 things that actually help when you're overstimulated:"
- Visual pattern interrupt: hands pouring product in slow motion, unusual close-up

**What converts:**
- UGC/creator style beats studio production. Always.
- Real person on camera speaking directly to viewer. Eye contact. Authentic pacing.
- Text overlay with key words from the voiceover (accessibility + muted view)
- Trending audio in Spark Ads (use licensed or platform-safe tracks)
- 71% of watch/scroll decisions happen in the first 3 seconds

**What kills performance:**
- Logo or brand name in the first 3 seconds
- Professional ad intro music (sounds like a commercial instantly)
- No movement in the first second
- Generic lifestyle B-roll with no human connection

---

### TikTok Spark Ads (Boosted Organic)

**When to use:** Warm audiences, trust-building, when an organic post is already performing.

**Why it works:** Looks 100% organic. Comments, likes, and shares stay on the post. Users can visit the creator's profile. Completion rate is 30% higher than standard in-feed ads. Engagement boost of 142%.

**Process:** Post organically first. Watch for early engagement signals. Boost posts that get genuine saves, comments, or shares within 24 hours.

**Creative strategy:** Same as organic TikTok content. The "ad" is that it reaches more people — the content itself should not feel like an ad.

## Value-First Content Framework

> **The rule:** The content earns their attention by giving them something — a feeling, a lesson, a validation — before the product enters the picture. The product is the answer to a question they're already asking.

### Why It Works

- 70–80% of users ignore content that reads as advertising
- Inbound/value-first content generates 54% more leads at 62% lower acquisition cost
- In wellness/spiritual niches specifically: users trust education and authentic testimony over claims

### The Structure (Applies to All Platforms)

```
1. MEET THEM WHERE THEY ARE
   Name a feeling, situation, or frustration they already have.
   Do not introduce the product yet.

2. TEACH OR VALIDATE
   Give them something useful. A tip, a reframe, a piece of information.
   Make them feel understood. Make them think "yes, that's exactly it."

3. THE NATURAL BRIDGE
   Connect what they just learned/felt to the product — as a tool, not a pitch.
   "This is what helped me with that."

4. SOFT CLOSE
   One clear action. Low pressure. "I'll link it." / "You can find it below."
```

### Value-First by Platform

| Platform | Lead with | Bridge phrase | CTA style |
|---|---|---|---|
| FB Image | Emotional copy — name the feeling in the headline | "Made for this exact thing." | Shop Now button |
| FB Video | Relatable moment or problem (0–10s) | Natural product reveal | "Find it here" + Shop button |
| IG Feed | Lifestyle visual that reflects their identity | Caption connects to product | "Link in bio" or swipe-up |
| IG Reels | Creator POV — "here's what I do" | Product appears as part of their routine | "Where I got mine" style |
| TikTok In-Feed | Education, list, or surprising claim | "This is where that changed for me" | "Linked below" + Shop CTA |
| TikTok Spark | Organic-feeling testimonial or ritual content | Product is part of the story naturally | Boosted post — same CTA |

## Salvia Lion — Specific Application Notes

> These apply the general framework to this specific client. Update per client at the top of a new section.

**Target feeling:** Overstimulated, energetically drained, emotionally porous. Empaths who absorb the room.

**What they want to feel after buying:** Cleared. Grounded. Like they gave themselves something real.

**Avoid:** New-age jargon that feels performative. Spiritual gatekeeping. Overpromising. Words like "manifest", "high vibe", "frequency" used without context.

**Lean into:** The sensory experience (smell, warmth, texture). The ritual of it. The science-adjacent side (minerals, the nervous system). Feeling like a normal self-care practice, not an niche spiritual product.

**Best-performing angles based on niche research:**
1. "You absorb energy from everyone around you. Here's what helps." (validation → product)
2. "What I put in my bath when the week was too much." (ritual → product)
3. "The ingredient in these that I didn't expect to care about." (education → product)
4. "Made by hand. Charged with intention. For people who feel everything." (identity → product)

**Tone:** Quiet, warm, confident. Not selling. Sharing.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why it fails | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Logo in first 3 seconds | Signals "ad" immediately — users scroll | Start with the hook. Brand comes later. |
| Polished studio footage on TikTok | Looks like a commercial, gets scrolled | Lo-fi, handheld, real-person feel |
| Hard sell in the first half | Breaks trust before it's established | Teach or validate first |
| Generic lifestyle visuals | No human connection, forgettable | Put a person in the frame |
| No text overlay on video | Muted scrollers miss the message | Captions + key text overlays |
| Same creative running 30+ days | Algorithm fatigue, CTR drops | Rotate every 2–3 weeks |
| "Link in bio" in a paid ad | Users won't navigate there | Use the platform's CTA button |
