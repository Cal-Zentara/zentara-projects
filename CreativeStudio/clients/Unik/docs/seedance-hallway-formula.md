# UNIK Seedance Hallway Formula (v5/v6)

The locked recipe for the hallway ad that worked. Camcorder look is the anti-AI-slop lever — it makes the footage feel real and found, not generated.

## Why it works
- **Sony Handycam DCR-TRV camcorder aesthetic** — CCD sensor color, auto-exposure hunting, warm fluorescent cast, soft digital noise, motion blur, handheld wobble. This is the single biggest lever. It reads as a real person catching their friends, not a crew.
- **Multiple kids in different colored polos** — shows the product range ("every color") inside the story.
- **VO is short and casual** — "School is 6 weeks away. New year, new fits. Unique, school uniforms for every kid."
- **Playful lens tap ending** — breaks the fourth wall, feels candid.

## Settings
- Seedance 2.0, 9:16, 480p, 15s duration, generate_audio on
- Start image: hallway frame

## The boys prompt (v5/v6 — locked, works)

Sony Handycam DCR-TRV consumer camcorder, CCD sensor color, auto-exposure slightly hunting, warm color cast under fluorescent lights, soft digital noise, slight motion blur on movement. Blue metal lockers on both sides, terrazzo floor, long hallway depth. Handheld wobble throughout — not a crew, just someone catching their friends.

Shot 1 (0-4s): Camera drifts forward slowly. Royal blue polo boy and burgundy polo boy walk ahead maybe 15 feet out, talking to each other. Neither looks back. Slight handheld wobble as the camera follows. Hallway depth pulls away behind them. Lockers line both sides in soft blur. Fluorescent light catches the royal blue polo from behind. Their backpacks sway with each step. Camera stays back and watches. Ambient hallway noise — muffled voices, a locker somewhere.

Shot 2 (4-8s): Camera drops to knee height and tilts up. Royal blue polo boy glances back over his left shoulder mid-stride, just for a second, then turns forward. Slight handheld wobble as the camera adjusts. Camera holds him in frame as he keeps walking. Royal blue polo bright against the lockers behind him. Backpack strap on his shoulder. Warm light, gentle grain. Camera stays low as he walks out of frame.

Shot 3 (8-11s): Camera finds the Black boy in the white long-sleeve polo at his locker from the side. One arm reaching up into the open locker. His curly afro catches the soft window light from the end of the hall. Polo stretches as he reaches. He pulls something out, turns away, lets the locker swing half-shut behind him. Warm female voice over begins: School is 6 weeks away. New year, new fits.

Shot 4 (11-15s): Black polo boy walks toward the camera at an easy confident pace. Camera at chest height, slight handheld drift, full uniform in frame — black polo, khakis, sneakers. He glances down at his polo, brushes off the front, looks up at the lens with a smirk. Voice over continues: Unique, school uniforms for every kid. Right as the voice over ends he reaches out and gives the lens a playful palm tap. Frame shakes once and cuts to black.

Audio: sneakers on terrazzo floor, locker closing in distance, hallway voices low underneath, fabric rustle. Upbeat instrumental hip hop beat builds through the final shot.

## The boys prompt — Variant B (candid documentary, no lens tap)

Difference from above: early-2000s digicam look instead of Sony Handycam, and the kid never acknowledges the camera — he walks past the lens without slowing. Ends on the empty hallway. Feels more like real found footage, less performed.
