## The series
A deadpan nerd scientist films himself on his phone, UGC style, stress-testing UNIK kids' uniform pants. The pants are on a crash test dummy. Each test he tries harder to destroy them. They never break. He stays totally deadpan and gets quietly more unhinged.

**The joke:** insane events shot like cheap handheld phone footage. The contrast is the comedy. Same guy, same garage lab, every clip.

**Per-clip rhythm:** he looks at the phone, says "Test number X," runs the test, inspects the pants, deadpan reaction, scribbles on his clipboard.

## Format (locked)
- UGC, vertical 9:16, phone-shot look start to finish (handheld, grainy, real). NEVER switches to cinematic.
- ALL REAL, NO CARTOON (Cal, June 6). Every test is a believable real-world test, so it's funny AND it's true proof. No meteor, no dinosaur, nothing impossible.
- **ONE single Seedance generation (Cal, June 6).** All 3 beats live inside ONE ~15s multi-shot clip, not 3 separate clips. No stitching. Seedance handles multi-shot in one gen with time markers, and one gen keeps the scientist + dummy + pants consistent across all 3 beats. Cheaper too (one gen, not three).
  - 0 to 5s: pressure washer
  - 5 to 10s: knee slide (recess test)
  - 10 to 15s: bike drag (finale)
- **ONE location** (Cal, June 6): everything happens in his driveway/garage so the AI only has to hold one world together. Big quality win for a single multi-shot gen.
- Deadpan scientist = recurring locked character (reference prompt below).
- Crash test dummy wears the real UNIK pants. Product tested on a dummy, NEVER on a kid (hard safety line).

## Round 1 — the 3 beats (locked, order set by Cal June 6)
All real, NO animals, all in ONE location (his driveway/garage): water, then a kid-style abrasion test, then a drag.

1. **Pressure washer.** "Test one." Blasts the pants point blank with a pressure washer. Dirt and water fly off. Not a thread moves. (Safest beat to generate: simple water action, no animals, no violence, won't trip filters, and on brand because dirt washing off ties to durability.)
2. **Knee slide (the recess test).** "Test two." Drags the dummy across the driveway on its knees, like a kid sliding at recess. Knees scrape the concrete. No holes. (This is secretly the strongest beat: knees are where uniforms die, and the real Amazon review is literally "the only ones that didn't end the year with holes in the knees." The test IS the brand promise acted out.)
3. **Bike drag (finale).** "Test three." Ties the dummy to his bike, drags it down the street in a dust cloud. Picks the pants up. No tears. "...huh."

**End card (added in post, not generated):** "Built to survive a kid. 20 years. unik."
