# Mom Attention Playbook — How to Find More Moms

*Researched June 10, 2026 (5 parallel agents, sourced). Trigger: Egypt meme reach skewed male; Cal wants the audience to be moms. This doc is the permanent reference — do not re-research.*

---

## 1. Hooks that stop a mom's scroll (first 1-3 seconds)

- **POV + relatable chaos** — "POV: You're getting four kids out the door before 7am." Chaos/real content performs ~2x better than polished. (OpusClip 2026 hook data)
- **Specific numbers** — "14,000+ reviews, 4.2 stars" outperforms vague claims by ~60%. Numbers trigger involuntary attention.
- **Myth buster / contrarian** — "Nobody tells you this about school uniforms." The brain can't ignore an unresolved contradiction.
- **Question openers** — direct curiosity gap, 49% of top-performing content.
- **Imperative** — "Stop scrolling if you have kids in school."

**Text overlay hard rules:** 5-7 words max, bold high contrast, sans serif, TOP third of the screen (platform UI covers the bottom), ALWAYS present (most moms scroll muted — text beats audio-only by 40%).

**Timing:** visual hook 0-0.5s, verbal hook 0.3-1.5s, payoff setup by 3s. Most scrolls die in second 1.

## 2. Emotions that drive action (ranked)

1. **Feeling seen / validation** — names the specific struggle without judgment. Drives SHARES + SAVES. (The bus stop found message is this.)
2. **Guilt relief** — permission slips ("the mess means they played"). Drives SAVES.
3. **Authenticity / real mess** — moms are allergic to perfect. Drives ENGAGEMENT.
4. **Humor about chaos** — laugh WITH the madness, never at the kid. Drives SHARES / viral.
5. **Community ("you're not alone")** — "tag a mom who gets this." Drives COMMENTS.
6. **Nostalgia / kids growing fast** — bittersweet time passing. Drives SAVES.

Rule: relatability before authority. Advice only lands AFTER she feels seen.

## 3. What converts a mom into a buyer

- **93% of parents research online before buying kids' clothing** (ParentsandBrands). No impulse buys off one reel.
- **Other moms' words beat brand claims** — reviews and peer recommendations are the #1 proof. UNIK's 14,000+ reviews / 4.2★ is the biggest unused weapon.
- **Trust closes, price doesn't** — quality + durability proof first; price is the pleasant surprise (matches the locked UNIK message order).
- **Conversion takes 2-3 months of consistent presence** — younger parents (25-34) shop AFTER school starts too. The 10-week roadmap cadence is correct.
- **Founder/family authenticity beats hired influencers** — real family in the product (Kyte Baby model).
- Path: Instagram discovery → research (reviews) → unikusa.com / Amazon purchase.

## 4. Latina mom content culture (English-language)

- Humor style: **deadpan, character-driven, "I'm not complaining, I'm just saying"** — observation as confession, exaggerated physicality, real-life pacing. (Matches UNIK's winning format exactly.)
- Repeating themes: survival humor, family group chat / chisme, abuela energy, school logistics as comedy, kids growing up bicultural.
- **Latina moms make ~86% of Hispanic household purchase decisions** and respond to strong quality brands, not price-first messaging (EC Hispanic Media).
- Creator to study: **Kat Stickler** (Cuban American — turned Hispanic mom mannerisms into mainstream comedy). Also Vivi McCoyn (bilingual motherhood, 750K TikTok).
- NO uniform brand owns this audience. Wide open. (Confirms the original UNIK bet.)
- Spanish content: still blocked — no native speaker to vet. English only.

## 5. Formats that win with moms (ranked by job)

| Format | Job |

**Stolen hook shapes (from a viral-schedule carousel Cal found, June 11 — kept only the ones that work without a personal creator):**
- **Expectation vs Reality** — expectation: ugly uniforms, reality: actually cute. Same engine as the Week 2 Flip haul reel.
- **Before/After** — pants in September vs pants in June. The durability story in a new wrapper.
- **Mistakes** — "uniform shopping mistakes moms make." Save-worthy hook for the weekly carousel slot.
- **POV** — "POV: it's 7:42am and nobody is dressed." Mom POV is already half our comedy engine; the joke lives in the text hook so it works muted.
- **Unpopular Opinion** — "Unpopular opinion: uniforms are the best thing that ever happened to school mornings." Comment bait on a static, backup hook shape for the engagement slot.

## 6. The timing bomb

**"Back to school haul" searches peak in July** (all-time high July 2025, Google Trends). Prime window: **June 15 - August 15**. Post the haul-style and durability content hardest in this window.
