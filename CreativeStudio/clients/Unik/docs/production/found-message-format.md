# Found Message Format — Unik Version

Adapted June 10, 2026 from the universal playbook: `CreativeStudio/found-message-format.md` (read that for the full why). This file is the Unik-specific version. It is a recurring slot on the live roadmap: it owns the **Community single image slot, once a week**.

**One line pitch:** a single candid photo of ONE short line, written on a real surface from a school mom's world, like someone left it there for her to find.

**Why Unik needs this exact format (from the June 10 stats pull):** the account has ZERO shares ever. This format exists to get forwarded mom to mom. It names a feeling she carries but never says out loud, so she sends it to another mom. It is also the cheapest post we make (one line, one image), which keeps the 5 a week pace sustainable.

---

## The frame (never changes)

- ONE short line, broken into 3 to 5 stacked centered lines, BIG enough to read as a thumbnail.
- On a REAL, ordinary surface, in a real place, shot candid in natural light.
- No logo, no brand name, no hashtag baked into the image. The line stands alone.
- Single image = 4:5 tall (1080x1350). Never square, never a carousel.
- It does NOT sell. No product claims, no price, no CTA in the image.

## Unik's wells (the feelings — rotate, never two in a row)

1. **The unsung labor** — she keeps this kid clothed, fed, and on time, and nobody claps for it.
2. **School morning chaos** — the 7am scramble every mom knows and laughs about later.
3. **Pride watching them grow** — the uniform that fit in August doesn't fit in May, and that's the whole point.
4. **Real life mess** — grass stains, playground dirt, the knees that always go first. Mess means they played.
5. **Durability relief** — the quiet win of a thing that just holds up. (Feeling only. Never a product claim, never a price. Thuy's rule: nothing that isn't literally true, and this slot doesn't sell anyway.)
6. **The village** — moms helping moms, the group chat, the hand me down bag.

## Unik's surfaces (rotate, never the same twice in a row)

- A stack of folded uniforms on top of the dryer, note resting on it
- Marker on a torn piece of cardboard at a cluttered kitchen table
- Chalk on the playground blacktop
- Finger written in the dust on a school bus window
- Dry erase marker on a classroom whiteboard
- A sticky note stuck to a lunchbox
- Taped paper sign on a school hallway locker
- Sharpie on masking tape across a plastic storage bin of outgrown clothes

## One human touch (pick ONE per image, never a busy scene)

A mom's hand holding the note, a kid's backpack dumped in the corner of frame, morning light through a kitchen window, a small sneaker by the door, a lunchbox half packed. The touch sells "a real person left this."

## Voice rules (Thuy locked)

- Playful, deadpan, real, short. Reads like a mom texted it, not like a brand wrote it.
- NEVER overclaim. If it states a fact, it must be literally true. Feelings are safe; claims are not.
- No price, no "under $15," no product pitch. This slot builds the page, the other 4 weekly posts sell.
- No hyphens or dashes anywhere in the copy. Hard rule.
- Spanish lines only if vetted by an outside native speaker first. The family is Vietnamese and cannot check it.

## Starter lines (one per well, ready to build)

1. Unsung labor: `NOBODY CLAPS FOR THE MOM WHO FOLDED THE POLOS AGAIN. SO WE'RE CLAPPING.`
2. Morning chaos: `WE WERE ALMOST ON TIME TODAY. ALMOST COUNTS.`
3. Pride: `IT FIT IN AUGUST. IT DOESN'T FIT IN MAY. THAT WAS THE WHOLE JOB.`
4. Real life mess: `GRASS STAINS MEAN THEY PLAYED HARD TODAY.`
5. Durability relief: `THE KNEES ALWAYS GO FIRST. EVERY MOM KNOWS.`
6. The village: `SOMEBODY'S MOM SAVED YOUR MORNING WITH THE HAND ME DOWN BAG.`

A great line is reusable later if the surface changes (tiny sticky note one month, chalk across the whole blacktop another).

## Prompt shape (gpt_image_2 — best text rendering. Swap line + surface + touch each week)

```
Photorealistic candid photograph, vertical 4:5 portrait, warm natural morning light. A stack of freshly folded school uniform polos on top of a white dryer in a real lived in laundry room, with a handwritten note resting against the stack that reads "NOBODY CLAPS FOR THE MOM WHO FOLDED THE POLOS AGAIN. SO WE'RE CLAPPING." in big handwritten marker capital letters, large and easy to read, broken across four stacked centered lines. A small kid's sneaker sits on the floor at the edge of frame. Real and candid like a photo a friend took, authentic textures, slightly uneven light, shallow depth of field, no logos, no other text anywhere. Vertical 4:5.
```

## Production rules (locked)

- Model: **gpt_image_2**, 4:5 portrait. Write the EXACT line into the prompt in quotes.
- Keep it REAL. No designed or branded look, or the "found" magic dies.
- If the brand mark is wanted: small lowercase "unik" watermark in a corner added in POST, never in the gen.
- Caption: one short playful line, Thuy's caption rule applies. No CTA needed; this post's job is the share.
- Write the prompt and STOP. Never generate without Cal's explicit "go / run it / fire it."

## Scorekeeping (stats first rule)

This is a page builder, not a reach play. Judge it on **saves and shares**, same as carousels. Baseline to beat: the account's first share, ever. If a found message gets the account's first share from a real mom, this slot earned its place.
