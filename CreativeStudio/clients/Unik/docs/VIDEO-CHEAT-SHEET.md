# How to Make AI Videos With Fewer Mistakes
*Plain-English cheat sheet. Read this before any video gen.*

---

## The one-liner
**Plan it fully → keep it simple → voice last → roll a few times → keep the best → stop.**

---

## 1. Plan it all first
Pick the scene, the voice lines, the photos, the settings — everything — before you hit go. Don't change stuff halfway through.

## 2. One job per video
One clear moment. Don't cram everything into 15 seconds. Close-up product details go in photos, not the video.

## 3. Voice goes on LAST
Never put the voice in the AI video. It prints the words on screen as captions, makes the voice sound dead, and the kids act out whatever you say (say "run and play" and they'll run). Generate the video clean, add the voice after.

## 4. THE BIG ONE — why kids smile, and how to stop it
**Why it happens:** The AI copies the smile straight off the photo you feed it. If the photo shows a big grin, every frame grins. You CANNOT type your way out of it — "don't smile" does nothing, because the picture beats the words.

**The fix:** Give the kids a real task to focus on — a jammed locker, drawing, reading something. The second their attention is on the task instead of the camera, the posing stops.

**The complete fix (when you need their faces toward the camera):** Make new reference photos of the kids with calm, neutral faces. Then the words finally work.

> Smiling = the photo's fault. Fix it with a task, or with a calmer photo.

## 5. Keep the actions simple
No fiddly hand stuff — latches, zippers, fabric. That's where it melts and warps. Big simple moves only (a tug, a pull, a walk).

## 6. Roll the dice a few times
Same prompt gives a different video every time. Run it 2 or 3 times, keep the best one, then STOP. Don't rewrite the prompt after every single try — that just re-rolls everything.

## 7. Don't change what's locked
If you said 4 photos, it stays 4. If something's fighting the gen, flag it and decide — don't quietly swap it.
