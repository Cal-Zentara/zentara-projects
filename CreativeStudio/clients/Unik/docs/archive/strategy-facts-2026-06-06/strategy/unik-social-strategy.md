# INSTAGRAM

IG rewards polish and visuals. Stop the scroll in the first 2 seconds.

## What works
- Reels and carousels only
- Visual hook in the first 2 seconds: motion, contrast, pattern interrupt
- Kids in uniforms moving. Real moments. No catalog poses.
- Show the product through motion, never by making anyone stand still
- Price line at the end, not the beginning

## Algorithm signals
- 3-second hold rate is everything. 60%+ pushes it wider. Under 40% it dies.
- Saves and shares weighted heavily
- Private shares (DMs to a friend) are the biggest signal
- Post 3 to 4 Reels per week plus 2 carousels

**Post at:** 7 to 9am or 6 to 8pm
**Links:** bio only, never in caption

## IG Visual Hook Formats to Build

| Hook | What it looks like | Why it works |
|---|---|---|
| **Wild UGC** | Calm mom reviews uniform while disaster builds behind her | Chaos contrast stops the scroll. See `wild-ugc-format.md`. |
| **Mid-scene open** | Clip opens already inside a moment (jammed locker, morning chaos) | Viewer has to watch to figure out what is happening |
| **Before/after durability** | Same kid, same polo, Day 1 vs Day 180 | Sells quality without saying a word. No AI gen needed. |
| **POV hook** | "POV: You finally found a school uniform that does not fall apart by October" | Personal, immersive, mom sees herself in it |
| **Color swatch reveal** | Opens on one color, names it, fast cuts through every color | Uniform brands live and die on color options |
| **Texture close-up then pull back** | Opens tight on fabric or stitching, pulls back to full outfit | Quality reads in the first frame |
| **Pattern interrupt** | Three identical quick cuts, then something completely different on the fourth | Brain cannot ignore a break in pattern. 32% higher engagement than single-shot openers. |

## IG Priority Order
1. Wild UGC (already built, fire when ready)
2. Before/after durability (easiest to produce, no AI gen, straight to the quality positioning)
3. POV hook (low production, high conversion)
4. Color swatch reveal (good for product launches)
5. Texture close-up (good for product detail posts)
6. Mid-scene open (already doing this in existing videos)
7. Pattern interrupt (build once the format is proven elsewhere)

## Key Principles (Meta AI, June 5, 2026)
- **"The narrower you go, the more people relate."** Hyper-specific POV hooks beat broad ones. Not "POV: you found a uniform that lasts" but "POV: it is week 14 and every other kid's polo looks like a rag. Your kid's looks the same as September."
- **The fake hair case study.** A creator with 6k followers hit 147M views by overlaying a fake hair on screen in the first 1.5 seconds. Viewers tried to wipe their screen. That is pattern interrupt, and the best proof of how powerful it is.

## Screen Anomaly Ideas (zero credits)
The fake hair mechanic: something in the first 1.5 seconds that makes the viewer physically interact with their screen (wipe, tap, replay). The replay is what the algorithm reads as high intent.
- **Smudge wipe to logo reveal (FFmpeg only).** Overlay a semi-transparent smudge PNG on the opening 2 seconds. Wipe reveals the clean logo. Build it as a post step on existing finals.
- **"Is that real?" first frame on Wild UGC.** The opening shot should look like a real location for half a second before the viewer clocks the mom.

**Do NOT steal:** lint on the polo (opens with a "dirty uniform" association, wrong for a quality brand), or a scratch on the locker (too confusing in 1.5 seconds).

## What NOT to do on IG
- Glossy, high-polish catalog looks. Reads as AI or corporate.
- All static shots. Movement is required.
- Text overlays explaining the product. Show it, do not tell it.
- Held smiles at the camera. Reads as posed.
- Opening on a still frame. Motion in the first second or they are gone.

# FACEBOOK

FB rewards conversation. The caption does the work. The image just needs to not look cheap.

## What works
- Text posts, questions, native video
- Comment hook in the caption: a question or statement moms cannot ignore
- Short captions, 40 to 80 characters, front load the hook

## Algorithm signals
- Comments with 5+ words count 3x more than one-word replies
- Private shares (Messenger DMs) are the #1 signal, bigger than likes
- Write posts moms would forward to another mom
- Never say "like and share." FB penalizes engagement bait.

**Post at:** 12 to 1pm or 8 to 9pm
**Links:** always in the first comment, never in the caption. FB kills reach if the link is in the caption.

## The 4 Best FB Hook Types for UNIK

**1. Relatable struggle (no question needed).** Name the pain. Moms comment on their own.
- "My kid's polo is already pilling and it's week three."
- "Bought three of the same polo just to survive the school year."

**2. Specific experience question.** About their life, not the product.
- "How many polos do you go through before January?"
- "How much do you actually spend on back to school clothes?"

**3. Fill in the blank.** Lowest friction. People finish the sentence automatically.
- "The one thing I always end up rebuying for school is ___"
- "By October my kid's uniform always looks ___"

**4. Am I the only one.** Validation hook. Moms say same.
- "Am I the only one buying the same polo three times a year?"

## Full Range of FB Comment Hook Categories

| Category | What it is | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Identity call-out | Makes moms say "that's me" | "Only moms of boys will understand the knee holes by September" |
| Debate / pick a side | Drives arguments, both sides comment | "Navy vs white polo. There's a right answer. Fight me." |
| Fill in the blank | One blank, instant participation | "The smell that never comes out of uniforms is ___" |
| Pain confession | Name the struggle, invite horror stories | "Uniform horror story time. I'll go first." |
| Ask for advice | Moms love being the expert | "Help a new Kinder mom: how do you keep white polos white?" |
| Would you rather | Binary, one word to answer | "Would you rather spend $40 once or $15 three times?" |
| Tag a friend | Expands reach to friends of followers | "Tag the mom who's already on her third polo this year." |
| Hot take | Spicy but safe | "Cheap school uniforms are actually more expensive in the long run." |
| Number drop | Fast to answer, creates data | "How many polos did you buy last school year? Drop a number." |
| Caption this | Photo plus comment, double signal | Photo of stained polo: "Caption this. Best one wins." |
| Confession | Admit something real | "I bought the cheap ones this year. I already regret it." |
| Nostalgia | Shared memory, shareable | "Who else had to wear the same 2 polos Mon to Fri in elementary?" |

## Comment Laddering (the FB engagement method)

One post with 20 comment threads beats a post with 200 likes. FB counts thread depth, not surface reactions.

1. **Post a conversation starter.** One question moms can answer in one word or number.
2. **Reply to every comment within 60 minutes, with a question.** Never "thanks for sharing." End every reply with "?" so the thread keeps going.
3. **Pin your own first comment.** Go first to make it safe for others. "I'll go first: 4 polos. The playground ate two of them. What's your number?"

**FB engagement signals ranked:** comment threads (3+ replies to one comment) > saves > private shares > video watch time > likes (dead last).

## What NOT to do on FB
- Generic "What do you think?" or "Comment below." FB buries it.
- Links in the caption. First comment only.
- Long walls of text. Front load the hook, use line breaks.
- Selling anything in week 1. Build conversation first.

# TIKTOK

TikTok rewards watch time and sound, and it punishes polish. The more produced it looks, the less people trust it. Research saved June 5, 2026.

## What works
- The hook is watch time. Keep them past 3 seconds and the algorithm tests it wider.
- 70% completion rate is the bar. Below 40% it dies.
- Start mid-action. No slow intros, no logo reveals, no static product shots.

## TikTok Hook Types for UNIK

| Hook | Opening line |
|---|---|
| Outcome first | Show the kid in the uniform moving, no buildup |
| Contrarian | "Everyone says kids uniforms need to be cheap. Wrong." |
| Specific number | "14,000 reviews. 4.2 stars. Here's why." |
| Authority | "20 years dressing LA kids. Here's what we learned." |

## Sound is the #1 lever
- Trending audio in the first 48 hours of a trend gets 5 to 10x more views than posting after it peaks.
- The sound has to FIT the niche. A random trending sound on a uniform video sends mixed signals and hurts account health.
- Original audio that others stitch or duet is the biggest win. Your sound becomes the distribution vehicle.
- For UNIK: upbeat, warm, morning energy. Back-to-school confidence. Not fashion-industry or luxury sounds.

**How to find trending sounds:** TikTok mobile app only. Tap search, tap the music note icon. Or while creating: tap Add Sound, then the Trending tab. The desktop version does not show this.

## Algorithm signals
- Shares and saves are weighted 5x more than likes
- Rewatches are the strongest signal of all
- Niche consistency matters. Posting across unrelated topics drops reach ~45%.

## The TikTok play for UNIK
Build around evergreen formats (relatable mom moments, before/after, POV, product in motion, hauls, useful tips), then layer trending audio on top. Sound changes weekly, the content formula stays the same. Evergreen does not mean boring: it still has to entertain or teach. Wild UGC works here because the disaster is the entertainment and the uniform is evergreen.
