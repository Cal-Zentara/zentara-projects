## ⭐ THE FRAME (locked by Cal, June 10 2026)

**We don't sell nails. We sell the confidence she carries out the door.**

- The blocker is guilt: "spending on myself is selfish." (Research, June 10: self-care guilt is the #1 booking blocker for moms/nurses/caregivers.)
- The product is confidence: catching your own hands on the steering wheel, feeling put together, "I'm taking care of myself."
- Every post does one of two things: **removes the guilt** or **shows the confidence.** If a post does neither, it doesn't fit the roadmap.
- Never say the word confidence at her — show the moment she feels it. (Same shown-not-said rule as the Vietnamese-owned differentiator.)

## ⭐ THE HOOK RULE (locked by Cal, June 10 2026)

**Every post must answer three questions BEFORE it gets built:**
1. **Who are we calling out?** (the girl who rips her gel off, the mom who keeps canceling on herself, the one with 47 screenshots)
2. **What emotion spikes?** (caught, seen, defensive, curious, laughing — a SPIKE, not a warm hum. Warm doesn't stop a cold scroller; warm gets shared by people who already stopped)
3. **Why does SHE stop?** (because the post is about HER, not about us)

If Claude can't name the callout and the emotion in one line each, the post doesn't get built. Every post pitch comes with its callout + emotion stated.

## What the algorithm rewards (5-agent web research pull, June 10 2026 — source named per line)

- **Shares > saves > comments > likes.** Shares are the top growth signal (confirmed via Instagram's Adam Mosseri, cited by Later). Pretty-photo likes are the weakest signal.
- **One job per post.** Like = pretty reveal. Share = relatable or funny ("this is so us"). Save = useful tip or aspirational set. Book = trust + one-tap link. Never expect one post to do all four (Later, This Is Crowd, Awkwardly Social).
- **Reels get you FOUND, carousels get you BOOKED, single images are the weakest format** (CreatorFlow/Pacewalk — vendor-leaning, directionally consistent across sources). Exception: the found message single image — its job is shares via the line, not reach via format.
- **The real front door is Google, not Instagram.** Roughly 70 to 80% of salon clients start with a Google search; 97% read reviews first (BrightLocal 2024 survey — the most solid stat in the pull). Instagram is the "are they good" gut check. Keep Dalena's Google Business Profile stacked with recent real photos + reviews.
- **Trending audio within 24 to 48 hours of a sound rising = 3 to 5x more reach** (SocialGPT/HeyOrca). Never lock a song early; pull Instagram's @creators "IG Anthems" highlight the day of posting.
- **Self-care guilt is the #1 booking blocker** for moms, nurses, caregivers. NEVER use "treat yourself queen / you deserve it" (reads cheap and manipulative — Beall Research). Instead NAME the guilt and remove it: "an hour where nobody needs anything from you." The found message slot runs on this.
- **The competitor lane is empty** (June 10 pull): big OC accounts are coastal (Newport/Irvine, European-mani niche); Stanton/GG/Westminster salons live on Yelp with no IG voice. Hyperlocal Stanton identity + warm Vietnamese-owned story = two open flags nobody holds. Geotag + name Stanton/Beach Blvd every post.

## Post formats that build trust / engagement with NO filming

| Format | What it is | Why it works |
|---|---|---|
| **Myth buster graphic** | One bold true nail fact, e.g. "MYTH: gel ruins your nails. TRUTH: bad removal does." | Makes her the expert without bragging. No salon claim to verify. |
| **This vs That carousel** | Compare two services with real photos, e.g. BIAB vs Gel-X (which lasts longer). | Educational = saves. Carousels get more reach than single photos. |
| **Inspo board carousel** | 5 of her real sets, last slide "comment your number". | Clients screenshot for their next appointment = bookings. |
| **iMessage screenshot graphic** | A happy client text styled like a text message + the nail photo. | Reads as real proof, not salesy. |
| **Aesthetic price menu carousel** | Summer menu, services + icons + close-up hand photos. | Price transparency builds trust; menus get shared to Stories. |

Build all of these FREE in HTML (not Canva — banned). Always ship with download buttons (see decisions.md).

## Music — what makes women feel confident (5-agent research, June 12 2026)

- **Confidence music 2026 = quiet authority, not hype.** Beauty/self care content runs on smooth R&B, neo soul, lo-fi soft indie, "calm classy cinematic" sounds. The pop banger era is over for this niche (Epidemic Sound 2026 trends, SocialBee June 2026 reels report, Soundverse 2026).
- **Bass = confidence, scientifically.** Bass-heavy music measurably makes listeners feel more powerful — it's the bass itself, tested on instrumentals with bass manipulated up/down (Kellogg School/Northwestern 2014, Social Psychology and Personality Science). Put a deep warm bassline under any confidence content.
- **60 to 80 BPM relaxes and makes customers linger; slow music lifted sales 38% in the most replicated retail study** (Milliman 1982, Journal of Marketing Research). Fast music rushes people out. In-salon music should sit at 60 to 80 BPM, content music ~85 to 95.
- **Real women on salon sound** (Reddit r/Nails, r/AskSeattle + top Spotify salon playlists): soft soothing piano/strings/acoustic/lounge wins inside the salon; no EDM or hype tracks appear in any popular nail salon playlist. Clean lyrics is a baseline, not a differentiator.
- **Famous "Weightless reduces stress 65%" claim is NOT peer reviewed** (traces to a 2010 branded survey) — don't quote it in content.
- **Two Suno tracks to generate (prompts in transcript June 12):** "The Salon Song" (72 bpm felt piano warmth, in-shop and soft content) + "The Confidence Song" (88 bpm neo soul, deep warm bass, for reels/reveals). Suno: custom mode, lyrics blank, "no vocals, pure instrumental."
