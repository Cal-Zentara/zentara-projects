# Found Message Format — Nail'd It Spa Version

Adapted June 10, 2026 from the universal playbook: `CreativeStudio/found-message-format.md` (read that for the full why). This file is the Nail'd It specific version. It is a recurring slot on the live roadmap: it owns the **Community single image slot, once a week** (the feel seen, share bait post — it builds the page and does not sell).

**One line pitch:** a single candid photo of ONE short line, written on a real surface from a nail client's world, like someone left it there for her to find on the way to her appointment.

**Why Nail'd It needs this exact format:** the account's growth play is hyperlocal Stanton discovery plus feeling seen (the big OC nail accounts are all coastal and all reveal-only). This format names the feeling a client carries but never says out loud — the hour that belongs to her, the guilt of spending on herself — so she sends it to a friend. A share is a free introduction to a new local follower. It is also the cheapest post of the week (one line, one image), which keeps the 5 a week pace sustainable.

---

## The frame (never changes)

- ONE short line, broken into 3 to 5 stacked centered lines, BIG enough to read as a thumbnail.
- On a REAL, ordinary surface, in a real place, shot candid in natural light.
- No logo, no brand name, no hashtag baked into the image. The line stands alone.
- Single image = 4:5 tall (1080x1350). Never square, never a carousel.
- It does NOT sell. No service names, no prices, no booking CTA in the image.

## Nail'd It's wells (the feelings — rotate, never two in a row)

1. **The hour that's yours** — one hour where nobody needs anything from you. Not selfish. Maintenance. (From the brand voice work: nobody books because their nails are bad, they book for the hour.)
2. **Permission to spend on yourself** — she pays for everyone's everything and feels guilt the second it's her turn. This well gives permission.
3. **Catching your own hands** — the quiet confidence after a fresh set: the steering wheel glance, typing slower so you can watch, hands that look expensive.
4. **A place that remembers you** — your usual chair, your usual color, being known by name. The Vietnamese family warmth, shown never said. Pairs with the hyperlocal Stanton lane.
5. **Hard working hands** — her hands carry kids, keyboards, groceries, everyone. They earned care too. (Moms, nurses, teachers — the actual Stanton clientele.)
6. **Fresh set joy, with humor** — set day as the small thing holding the week together. Chipping a nail on day two is real heartbreak. Gentle, deadpan, never mean.

## Nail'd It's surfaces (rotate, never the same twice in a row)

- A handwritten note resting against a wall rack of polish bottles in rainbow rows
- Finger written in the steam on a glass salon door, early morning
- Marker on the back of an appointment reminder card held in fingers
- Chalk on the sidewalk outside a sunlit strip mall storefront
- A sticky note stuck to the corner of a salon station mirror
- A torn note tucked under a polish bottle on a manicure table
- Marker on an iced coffee cup sleeve on a salon side table
- A note pinned to a small corkboard by a register
- Finger written in the fog of a bathroom mirror at home, getting ready
- A paper sign taped inside a shop window, shot from the sidewalk

## One human touch (pick ONE per image, never a busy scene)

Warm window light across the note, a blurred figure walking past outside the glass, an iced coffee sweating next to the note, steam rising off a pedicure bowl in soft focus behind it, a cardigan sleeve at the edge of frame, a row of polish bottles catching the light.

**⚠️ Brand specific rule on hands: if a hand appears, nails stay bare or barely visible.** Never show detailed painted nail art in a generated image — Dalena's brand is real nail photos only, and AI nail art on her account (even in a candid prop hand) is the one thing that can break it. The safe human touches are light, props, and figures, not manicures.

## Voice rules (from the brand doc — Dalena locked)

- Warm but not soft. Confident, never salesy. Reads like a trusted nail tech left you a note, not like a brand wrote a caption.
- Plain talk: never "utilize," "ensure," "we strive," "pamper yourself," "treat yourself queen."
- Feelings are safe, claims are not. No service promises, no prices in this slot.
- No hyphens or dashes anywhere in the copy. Hard rule.
- English lines only unless a Vietnamese line is vetted by Dalena herself first (she can check it — use this sparingly, for Tết season it could be special).

## Starter lines (one per well, ready to build)

1. The hour that's yours: `ONE HOUR WHERE NOBODY NEEDS YOU. THAT'S NOT SELFISH. THAT'S MAINTENANCE.`
2. Permission: `YOU FIXED EVERYONE'S WEEK. SOMEBODY SHOULD FIX YOUR NAILS.`
3. Catching your hands: `YOU'LL CATCH YOURSELF STARING AT YOUR OWN HANDS. LET IT HAPPEN.`
4. A place that remembers: `SOMEWHERE IN STANTON THERE'S A CHAIR THAT KNOWS YOUR NAME.`
5. Hard working hands: `THESE HANDS CARRY EVERYTHING. LET SOMEONE HOLD THEM FOR AN HOUR.`
6. Fresh set joy: `BREAKING A NAIL ON DAY TWO IS A REAL HEARTBREAK. WE SAID WHAT WE SAID.`

A great line is reusable later if the surface changes (sticky note on the mirror one month, chalk across the whole sidewalk another).

## Prompt shape (gpt_image_2 — best text rendering. Swap line + surface + touch each week)

```
Photorealistic candid photograph, vertical 4:5 portrait, warm late afternoon light. A handwritten note in big marker capital letters resting against a wall rack of nail polish bottles arranged in rainbow rows in a real lived in nail salon, the note reads "ONE HOUR WHERE NOBODY NEEDS YOU. THAT'S NOT SELFISH. THAT'S MAINTENANCE." large and easy to read, broken across four stacked centered lines. Warm window light falls across the note, polish bottles catching small glints of light, real and candid like a photo a friend took, authentic textures, slightly uneven light, shallow depth of field, no logos, no other text anywhere. Vertical 4:5.
```

## Production rules (locked)

- Model: **gpt_image_2**, 4:5 portrait. Write the EXACT line into the prompt in quotes.
- Keep it REAL. No designed or branded look, or the "found" magic dies.
- If the brand mark is wanted: small watermark in a corner added in POST, never in the gen.
- Caption: one short warm line in Dalena's voice. No booking CTA needed; this post's job is the share. (CreatorFlow still catches any "BOOK" comments it earns on its own.)
- Write the prompt and STOP. Never generate without Cal's explicit "go / run it / fire it."

## Scorekeeping

This is a page builder, not a reach play. Judge it on **saves and shares**. If a found message gets sent from one Stanton local to another, this slot earned its place.
