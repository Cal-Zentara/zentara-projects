Brand concept:
Garlic High serves Jiro-style ramen — a Tokyo-born bowl built on extra-thick noodles, rich all-day pork broth, and fresh garlic piled on with zero restraint. This ad introduces the concept to a Gen Z audience in bold fast-food commercial energy: bright yellow, low-angle hero shots, food as the star, punchy and confident.

Visual style:
Primary background is flat saturated yellow — consistent across every panel, no gradients, no environments. Product lighting is warm and appetite-forward: soft front key light, slight warm rim. Camera angles are LOW — most shots look up at the food, making it feel massive and powerful like a real fast-food commercial. Bold black 3D block typography appears in panels 01, 02, and 08. "MORE GARLIC POR FAVOR" appears as bold oversized text overlay in panel 04 only.

Video intent:
Each panel suggests a distinct shot in a 14-second vertical commercial split into two 7-second halves. Panels 1-4 are the intro and energy section. Panels 5-8 are the ingredients and brand close. Fast-food pacing — each frame is a distinct visual punch.

Storyboard format:
One clean 8-panel contact sheet arranged in 2 rows of 4 panels. Consistent spacing, subtle separation between panels. No frame numbers, captions, arrows, or annotation marks. Reads left to right, then top to bottom.

Panel aspect-ratio rule:
Every individual panel must be framed as a distinct 9:16 vertical shot. Tall, mobile-first. No horizontal, square, or landscape panels inside the grid.

Story arc:
Opens with a clean brand title card, reveals the bowl from a powerful low angle, delivers the money shot, hits the garlic tagline over the cascade, then breaks down the ingredients and closes on the brand stamp.
