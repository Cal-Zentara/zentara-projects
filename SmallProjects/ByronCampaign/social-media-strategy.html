🗓 May 9 Fundraiser — Your Launch Moment
The fundraiser is the first major content event. Post Byron's story before it. Capture reels and stories during it. Drop a recap post after it. This is how you go from 39 followers to your first 200.
1
What to Set Up Before Posting
Update both bios — IG and Facebook
Who he is, what he's running for, and the district. Example: "Running for Irvine City Council District 5. Affordability · Safety · Community."
Professional headshot as profile photo on both
Clean background, good lighting. First impression voters get before they read anything.
Add a link in bio (free Linktree is fine)
Send people somewhere — fundraiser RSVP, donation page, or a simple "learn more" page. Always have a place to send traffic.
Create a Facebook Event for May 9
If not already live, post the event immediately. FB events get natural reach in local community feeds.
Build one Canva template with Byron's branding
His name, colors, and font set once. Every graphic you make after that looks consistent and professional. Use it for every post.
Gather 3–5 photos of Byron
At minimum: one clean headshot + a few candid community shots. You need these before the first post goes out.

Month-by-Month Game Plan
May Launch
Goal: Get Byron's name out
  • Post Byron's story — who he is, why he's running, why District 5 matters to him
  • May 9 fundraiser: stories + reels from the event, show the room and energy
  • Post-fundraiser recap: "Here's what people said" momentum post
  • Content mix: 80% Byron's story, 20% issues
3x/week IG 4x/week FB Target: 150–200 followers
June Build Trust
Goal: People know him — now they need to like him
  • Behind-the-scenes content — Byron at community events, around District 5
  • Introduce issues one at a time, start with Safety or Community (easiest emotional connection)
  • Show up at local events and post about it — be visible in the community
3x/week IG 4–5x/week FB Target: 400–500 followers
July–Aug Issue Authority
Goal: Byron becomes the informed, prepared candidate
  • Rotate through all 5 platform pillars — one issue per week
  • "Did you know about District 5?" posts — local stats, real problems, Byron's answer
  • Carousel posts work great here — educational, easy to save and share
  • Mix in community event coverage to keep it human, not just political
3–4x/week IG Daily FB Target: 800–1,000 followers
September Persuasion
Goal: Turn followers into voters
  • Testimonials — neighbors, community members, local voices who support Byron
  • Byron at events — shows up, shows out. Post every appearance
  • Address common doubts ("why change what's working?" — his direct answer)
  • Ramp up Reels — IG pushes these hardest to non-followers
4x/week IG Daily FB Target: 1,500+ followers
October Mobilize
Goal: Get people ready to vote
  • Post voter registration deadlines clearly — dates and links
  • Early voting dates and locations in District 5
  • "Here's exactly how to vote for Byron" — step by step, no guessing
  • High energy, urgent tone — election is weeks away
Daily both platforms
November Final Push
Goal: Get out the vote
  • Every post is a reminder to vote — polls open, polls closing, deadlines
  • Election day: real-time stories throughout the day
  • Thank you post on election night regardless of result — community goodwill lasts
Daily + multiple stories per day

What Types of Posts to Make
Reels (IG) — Highest Priority
Instagram pushes Reels to non-followers. This is how you grow. Even 15–30 second clips of Byron speaking, at events, or quick issue explainers work. Aim for at least 1 Reel per week.
Carousels (IG) — Issue Posts
Multi-slide posts for explaining the 5 pillars. Easy to read, easy to save. Great for "Did you know about District 5?" content. People swipe through and share.
Stories — Daily Presence
Behind-the-scenes, polls, "swipe up" to RSVP or donate. Stories don't need to be polished — raw and real works best. Post daily once you're in the final push.
Facebook Events + Groups
Create FB Events for every fundraiser and appearance. Share posts into local District 5 community groups — this is where Irvine residents actually talk. Most impactful FB move.
Static Graphics — Announcements
Event announcements, quotes from Byron, voter deadline reminders. Use the Canva template for these. Quick to make, clean, consistent look across every post.
The 80/20 Rule — Always
80% valuable content (story, community, education, issues) and 20% asks (donate, share, come to the fundraiser). If every post is an ask, people tune out.
