How We Build Your Audience

You do not grow by posting at the people who already follow you. You grow by reaching new moms, making them feel something, and then actually talking to them. Three moving parts. That is the whole machine.

1

Great content

Posts that make the right mom stop and feel seen. Already built into your plan.

2

Reach

Short video that the app shows to moms who do not follow you yet.

3

Connection

Reply to every comment and DM. Turn a viewer into a customer who tells her friends.

01

Play where the moms are

From your own research.

  • Instagram and Facebook are home. That is where Latina moms actually live and shop.
  • TikTok is your reach bonus. It finds brand new people fast, so it is worth it even if she buys on Instagram.
  • Same video, posted to all of them. Wait a day before reposting to TikTok so it does not get flagged as a repeat.
02

The engine: short video with a killer hook

This is the part that reaches strangers. Get this right and everything works.

  • The first 2 seconds decide everything. If she does not stop, nothing else matters.
  • Keep videos short, about 20 to 35 seconds. She is busy. Let her watch the whole thing.
  • Build for saves and shares, not likes. A save means "I need this later." A share means "my friend needs this." Those two are what the app rewards with reach.
  • Use the hooks you already locked: the price shock, the 7am dryer panic, the bilingual punch.
Before any post goes out, ask one question: why would a mom save this or send it to another mom? If there is no answer, it is not ready.
03

The rhythm: 3 a week, batched

Matches your roadmap. Sustainable, not burnout.

  • 3 posts a week: one helpful, one that builds trust, one that sells. You already planned this.
  • Batch it. Make a whole week or month in one sitting, then space them out. This is how you keep up without it eating you alive.
  • Consistency beats volume. 3 good ones every week beats 7 rushed ones some weeks.
04

Turning the dials: extra reach

Small levers that push you in front of more new moms.

  • Trending sounds on TikTok give a real boost, but only if the sound fits. Jump on them early, within a day or two.
  • Captions are search. Write plainly what is on screen ("school uniform polos that survive the whole year"). Moms search these words.
  • Reply to comments with a video. The app shows that reply to everyone who watched the first one. Free reach.
  • Make a series. A repeating format ("uniform durability test, day 1, day 2...") brings people back and tells the app you are reliable.
05

Connection: turn watchers into buyers

This is where followers become customers who refer their friends.

  • Reply to every comment fast, ideally within the hour. Fast replies get the post shown to more people.
  • When someone asks "where do I buy?", reply and send them to the UNIK webpage, not Amazon. The webpage is the channel they own.
  • You already built comment to DM automation for other clients. Point it here.
  • Treat every DM like a hot lead. A real person, a quick warm reply, a soft nudge to the link.
06

The biggest multiplier: real moms

Your plan saves this for later. When you are ready, it is the single strongest lever.

  • Real moms posting your uniforms beat polished brand content by a mile. It looks true, because it is.
  • Give free uniforms to a handful of real LA moms with small followings. Ask to repost them.
  • Make a branded tag (like #unikkids). When customers post, repost them to Stories and tag them. That makes more moms want to post.
07

A little money, only on winners

Paid does not save a flop. It pours gas on a fire that is already lit.

  • When a post is already doing well on its own, put $10 to $30 behind it. That can multiply who sees it.
  • Never boost a post that is flopping. The app already decided. Save your money.
  • Your plan stays organic only until something proves itself. That is the right call.
08

What's actually working right now

Verified June 2026 from real posts on Instagram. This is the path from scroll to sale. Each row is one video.

  • Stop the scroll — a relatable kid-chaos hook with a fast payoff. Example that worked: "My 7yo said uniforms are boring... until she styled this," then cut to her in the polo and skirt. Real version of this ran April 24, 2026.
  • Build trust — handheld, unscripted, show the uniform surviving real life. "Wrinkle-free, stain-resistant, and she can't rip it on the playground." Proof beats claims. This is UNIK's durability story, the same angle Old Navy's top post used.
  • Get shares — a sound parents already know plus a confident kid. "POV: you found uniforms she actually asks to wear." Parents tag their friends.
  • Get the click — price and guarantee in the first 3 seconds. "5-pack polos, free returns all year." Pin the link in the comments. Send them to the UNIK webpage, not Amazon.
The big opening: UNIK's two biggest competitors, French Toast and Lands' End, barely post on social at all. Old Navy is the only one showing up, and even their best posts only get a few hundred to about a thousand likes. The field is wide open. UNIK does not need to go viral. It just needs to show up consistently when no one else is.

The honest truth

This takes about 4 to 8 weeks of steady, good content before it clicks. Early on, a post getting a few hundred views is normal. Do not panic and do not quit.

The win you are after is not viral. It is a few hundred real LA moms who know the UNIK name and trust it before back to school. That is what turns into sales, and into moms telling other moms.

Ask every buyer one question: "How'd you hear about us?" That tells you what is actually working.
