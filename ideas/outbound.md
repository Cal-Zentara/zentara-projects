## MANYCHAT LEAD MAGNET — Instagram Automation (April 22, 2026)

Post a reel. Tell people to comment a keyword. Everything after that is automatic.

**How it works:**
1. Post any reel → end with "comment MOON (or any word) for my free guide"
2. ManyChat detects the comment → auto-DMs the person a link to a free Notion doc
3. 23 hours later → automated follow-up DM → link to buy or book

**The lead magnet (Notion doc):** Claude builds it in ~5 min in the client's voice. Value-first content, CTA at the bottom.

**Cost:** Free. ManyChat free plan covers this exact flow. Notion is free. The reel is the only manual part — and they're already posting reels anyway.

**Tested in the wild:** Someone used this and got 31 leads from one reel. Auto-comment reply + DM + follow-up all running on its own.

**Best fit for:** Any client already posting on Instagram/TikTok who doesn't have a system for capturing leads from their content. Especially service businesses — spiritual healers, coaches, salons, consultants.

**Dalena / Lavender Moon is the first pitch.** She's already active on TikTok, already has a $5.55 product and Square links live, already has an intake form. Everything's built — she just needs the automation layer on top.

## Zentara Outreach — Personal Cold Email System (April 17, 2026)

A personal version of Nirvana Marketing pointed at Cal's own bottleneck (outreach), not a client's. Same architecture, different niche.

**Target:** Small service businesses in Orange County — contractors first (plays to Cal's 14 years in construction), then salons, mortgage officers, real estate agents.

**The 5-step pipeline:**
1. **Find** — pull 20 businesses from Google Maps via Apify
2. **Research** — scrape each site + 1-2 star Google reviews
3. **Spot the pain** — AI surfaces one specific problem (broken booking, missed callbacks, no pricing page, Yahoo email, etc.)
4. **Write email** — 60-word teardown email naming that exact problem
5. **Queue to Gmail** — drafts sit in inbox, Cal reviews + sends

**4-touch follow-up arc (stolen from Nirvana):**
- Day 0: Teardown ("noticed X specific problem")
- Day 3: Benchmark ("here's how top 5 in your area handle it")
- Day 7: Case study ("did this for GRSI/Delena")
- Day 14: Breakup ("last email — close the file?")

**What already exists (~70% done via Nirvana):**
- Gmail sending via Nylas (Cal's grant already stored)
- Email-writing prompts + patterns
- 4-touch follow-up logic
- Reply handling (Interested / Not / Later)

**What's new (~30% to build):**
- Google Maps scraper for service businesses
- Contractor-specific pain-signal prompt
- Personal pipeline UI (or skip — use Google Sheet)

**The anchor pitch:** "14 years on crews before building software for guys in the trades." No competitor in AI-for-small-business can say this.

**Realistic math:** 100 emails → 50 opens → 5-10 replies → 2-5 calls → 1-2 clients. At $500 avg build, that's $500-$1,000 per 100 sends.

**Tooling ladder (don't skip steps):**
- 0-50/week → Manual Gmail drafts (free)
- 50-200/week → Google Workspace ($6/month)
- 200-1000/week → Instantly Growth ($37.60/month annual)
- 1000+/week → Instantly Hypergrowth ($77.60/month annual)

**Phase 1 — this week:** 10 contractors by hand. Manual drafts. No automation. See if the framework works before building anything. If nothing replies, automating wouldn't have saved it.

**Why not yet:** Cal's rule #6 — "the work you avoid is the work that pays." Building the system IS a way to avoid sending the emails. Do the manual 10 first.

## Skills to Learn — Facebook Ads

Learn to run ads for Cal.Zentara. Start from zero.

**The core problem:** Most people treat Facebook ads like a recipe. Follow a tutorial, set it up, when it stops working watch a new tutorial and reset everything. Constantly starting over instead of building on what's working.

**The fix — a system with 3 parts:**
1. Inputs — creative (ads), account structure, and settings
2. Process — analyze what's working, make more of it
3. Outputs — conversions, revenue

**Key insight:** Creative is both an input AND an output. Winning ads teach you what to make next. That's the flywheel.

**The 5 rules:**
1. **Rule of 10,000** — for every $10k/month in spend, upload that many new ads per week. Minimum 2/week. Spending $100/day ($3k/month)? Upload 2 new ads per week.
2. **Group ads by avatar and concept** — stay organized so you know what's actually working
3. **Go wide first, then deep** — test lots of different concepts, then when one wins, make 12 variations of that one ad
4. **Set a fixed upload day** — same day every week, ads go live
5. **Set a fixed analysis day** — deep dive into what worked, decide what to make next
