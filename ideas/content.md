## Personal Brand Strategy — Daniel Priestley Framework (April 18, 2026)

Starting Sunday. Three-part system for building a 7-figure personal brand.

**The core insight:** AI has read everything but lived nothing. Your lived experience is the only thing it can't replicate. Cal's story — 14 years construction → 6 weeks building with Claude → automating workflows for clients — is the asset.

**Step 1 — Find your stories**
Go through phone photos, last 60 months. Find moments that taught you something real. Write: what happened + what's the lesson for someone else. Look for *relatable*, not impressive. "Relatable beats impressive" (Steven Bartlett).

**Step 2 — Content format (Pain / Prize / News)**
Every post fits one:
- **Pain** — problem your audience keeps hitting
- **Prize** — what's possible when they get it right
- **News** — something happening in AI/Claude world + what it means for them

**Step 3 — The funnel**
- Short form (daily) → Pain/Prize/News post
- Long form (weekly) → one deeper piece: video, newsletter, or podcast
- CTA → "DM me" to start. No fancy system needed yet.

**Why personal brand over business brand:** Social media gives personal brands 20x the reach of business brands. Algorithm media (not social media) — it shows content by topic, not friendship. You don't need followers. You need the right topics.

**AIO (AI Optimization):** Content published now gets picked up by ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity. Someone asks an AI "how do I use Claude for my business" → it recommends your content. This window is open now, not in 2 years.

**Cal's starting setup:**
- Carousel = short form ✅ (needs Pain/Prize/News framing, not generic topics)
- Long form = missing ❌
- CTA = missing ❌

**First move Sunday:** Map 3 real stories from construction or first Claude builds → assign each one Pain, Prize, or News → build first week of posts from those.

## GEO — Generative Engine Optimization (April 15, 2026)

Getting your brand recommended by AI — not just ranked on Google.

**The big idea:** When someone asks ChatGPT or Perplexity a question, it pulls from websites it trusts. This is about how to make your site one of those sources.

**The 6 steps:**
1. **Build pages like Lego** — break content into clear sections with headings. AI scans for chunks it can grab, not walls of text. Each section should answer one specific question.
2. **Stop thin product pages** — if your page doesn't have enough detail, AI skips you and uses a competitor who does. Pack pages with specs, FAQs, testimonials, visuals, everything.
3. **Match how people actually prompt AI** — people don't type "winter jacket" into ChatGPT, they say "warmest jacket for Chicago winters under $300." Find those full questions by asking your support team or typing your category into Perplexity and seeing what follow-up questions it suggests.
4. **Give features their own pages** — a buried bullet point on a features list is weak. A dedicated page for each feature is a strong direct match when someone asks AI about it.
5. **Link your pages together** — connect everything so AI can see you cover a whole topic, not just one angle. Think of it as giving AI a table of contents for your expertise.
6. **Get mentioned outside your site** — Reddit, YouTube, LinkedIn, review sites, guest posts. AI trusts independent sources. The more places that mention you, the more AI recommends you.

**Why this matters for Cal.Zentara:** This is exactly how you'd get AI to recommend you when someone asks "who teaches people how to use Claude for their business."

## Short Form Video — Jenny Hoyos Framework (April 17, 2026)

Use this when building social content for Cal.Zentara.

**Structure (every video):**
1. Hook — visual first frame, understood without sound, explain like a 5-year-old
2. Foreshadow — 2 lines max, tell them what happens at the end
3. Smooth transition — never say "let's get started", flow into the story
4. But/therefore storytelling — story needs change, not "and then, and then"
5. Abrupt ending — cut the second it's over, not a frame later

**Target numbers:**
- ~34 seconds (find your own sweet spot through testing)
- 90%+ retention
- 85%+ scroll-through rate

**Platform differences:**
- YouTube Shorts — slower pace, more story, ~34 seconds
- TikTok — dense info, 10–20 seconds, less story
- Instagram Reels — visual, subtitles every second, highly shareable

**Key insight:** Retention alone doesn't determine virality. Viewer satisfaction does — rewatches and shares matter more than a perfect retention graph.

**Content angles for Cal.Zentara:**
- Real project builds (show the before/after)
- Surprising results ("I built this in 20 minutes")
- Relatable struggles ("it kept breaking until...")
- Client reactions
