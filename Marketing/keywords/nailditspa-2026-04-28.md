## Summary

The nail salon keyword space is mixed — big beauty brands (L'Oréal, Maybelline) own the generic how-to content, but local nail spas are actively ranking for pricing, comparison, and maintenance questions. That's the lane. The opportunity is to write 6–10 focused blog posts on the questions Dalena's actual clients ask before booking, and let those posts funnel people to her existing service pages.

## Quick Wins (Start Here)

1. **"how often should you get a nail fill"** — Small blogs and Quora are ranking for this. A proper post written from Dalena's actual experience as a nail tech will beat all of them. High relevance: this question is on every client's mind right before they book.

2. **"how much do acrylic nails cost"** — Local nail salons are ranking here. Writing a post that includes Nail'd It's actual pricing + why their quality is worth it turns a research query into a booking. Commercial intent — people googling this are about to spend money.

3. **"dip powder vs gel nails"** — Local nail spas are ranking here, including one in Orange County. This is a comparison search meaning the person hasn't decided yet — a helpful post that ends with "come in and we'll recommend what's right for your nails" is a soft pitch that converts.

---

## What to Skip

- **"acrylic vs gel nails which is better"** — L'Oréal Paris and big beauty publishers own the top 5. Not worth the effort right now.
- **"how to make nail polish last longer"** — Maybelline, L'Oréal, and product brands dominate. Wrong lane for a local nail salon.
- **"nail shapes guide"** — L'Oréal Paris, Marie Claire, and Bustle are ranking. Could revisit later with a local/visual angle but not a priority.
- **"nail art trends 2026"** (generic, no location) — Big beauty publishers hold this. The local version ("nail art trends 2026 Orange County") is the winnable angle instead.
