# Voice Profile — Salvia Lion (Nancy)
Last updated: April 27, 2026
Source: Discovery answers Q1–Q12 (her exact words)

---

## How She Writes

**Sentence length:** Mix. She'll write a long setup sentence, then land it with something short and final.
> "It took some time to figure it out but I'm confident enough now to know peoples' energies and anxiousness were affecting me at my last job." → "That one reiki session opened up a whole new world for me."

**How she opens:** Often with a memory or an observation — never with a pitch.
> "I remember at my last job..."
> "In the past, I've noticed that people barely starting their spiritual journey..."

**How she closes:** Clean and quiet. No fanfare.
> "That if I only reach 1 person, that's enough, that's plenty."
> "That's when I knew I wanted to help people."

## Her Patterns

| Pattern | Example |

| Humble qualifier | "I'd like to think..." / "I believe..." |

## Her Tone

- **Warm but not soft** — she cares deeply, but doesn't gush
- **Confident without bragging** — she knows what she does works, she just doesn't need to say it loudly
- **Empathetic first** — she leads with understanding what the person is going through, not what she offers
- **Non-pushy always** — never urgency, never "you need this," never hard sells
- **Spiritual without being inaccessible** — she explains energy work in plain language, not jargon
- **Personal** — she uses her own story as proof, not as performance

---

## What She Never Does

- Never uses urgency language ("limited time," "act fast," "don't miss out")
- Never overclaims ("cure," "fix," "guaranteed")
- Never writes in corporate-speak ("leverage," "utilize," "impactful")
- Never prescribes ("you should," "you need to," "you must")
- Never performs spirituality — she lives it, and that comes through in restraint

---

## Writing in Her Voice — Checklist

Before posting anything as Salvia, check:
- [ ] Does it sound like one real person talking to another?
- [ ] Does it open with empathy (what they're feeling) before the product?
- [ ] Is there at least one short, punchy sentence in it?
- [ ] Did you remove every word that could appear on a mass-market wellness brand?
- [ ] Would Nancy say this out loud without cringing?
