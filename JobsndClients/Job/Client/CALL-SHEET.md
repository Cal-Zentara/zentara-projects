Everything you need for today's calls on one page. Phones first, best leads first.

---

## Your one-liner (memorize this)

**To barbershops:** "I build websites for barbershops — one flat price, live within a week, gets you found on Google."
**To nail salons:** same line, swap in "nail salons."

**Goal of every call:** get a yes to *"Can I text you an example?"* That's it. Not selling the site on the phone.

---

## The script (30 seconds, then silence)

1. "Hi, is this [owner]? I'm Cal, I'm local here in Anaheim — I build websites for [barbershops/nail salons]."
2. "I went looking for [shop]'s website and couldn't find one — when someone googles '[barber/nails] near me,' the shops with a site show up first, and you don't."
3. "I build clean one-pagers — your work, your hours, a book-now button — so those searches land in your chair instead of someone else's."
4. "Can I text you an example? Takes ten seconds to look."
5. **Stop talking. Let them answer.**

If it's not the owner: "No worries — who handles that stuff, and when are they usually in?" Get a name, log it, move on.

## Questions to work in (pick 2–3)

1. "Where do most of your new customers find you right now?" → then: "and where do the ones who *search* end up?"
2. "How do people book — walk-in, call, Booksy?"
3. "Ever had a website before? What happened?"
4. "If you had five more new faces a week, could you handle them?"

## When they push back

| They say | You say |

| "Word of mouth is enough" | "That's why it'll work — you're already the shop people recommend. This catches the ones who *search* instead of ask." |
| "I've got Booksy / Instagram" | "Booksy ranks Booksy, not you. Google barely shows Instagram. Those are the back office — the website's the front door." |
| "Too expensive" | "One flat price, one time. Less than one chair makes on a Saturday — and it works every day after." |
| "My nephew's gonna do it" | "How long's that been the plan? I'll have it live by Friday — he can take the credit." |
| "Busy, not now" | "Totally — I'll just text the example. Ten seconds, no follow-up if you hate it. Best number?" |
