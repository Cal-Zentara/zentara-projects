### How to pitch these (phone call — keep it under 30 seconds, then ask for a yes)

**Master script (works for all 10 — swap the [brackets]):**
> "Hi [owner name / 'is the owner around?'] — I'm local here in Anaheim, I build websites for [barbershops/salons]. I noticed [Business] doesn't have one — so when someone googles '[barber/nails] near me,' the shops *with* a site get picked first and you're stuck on [Yelp/Booksy/Instagram]. I build clean one-pagers — your work, hours, and a book-now button — that get you found on Google. Can I text you an example to look at?"

**Per-lead personalization (say this instead of the generic gap line):**

1. **District Barbers (Danny):** "107 reviews and no website — you're busier than shops that *do* show up on Google."
2. **Anaheim Barbers II (Pete):** "26 years in business and no website — your reputation deserves to show up when people search."
3. **Hany's (Hany):** "You've got the Linktree and Instagram, but none of that shows up on Google when someone searches 'barber near me' in Little Arabia."
4. **The Projects (Nate & Will):** "You took over in 2022 and clearly leveled the shop up — a site is the missing piece for new walk-ins."
5. **Blvd (Eric & Sam):** "801 five-star reviews on Booksy and no website — Google has no idea how good you are."
6. **OC Barber:** "You do color, nails, and waxing too — but nobody googling finds that out. A site sells the bigger-ticket services."
7. **Major League:** "13 years strong, but only Facebook and Booksy — you're invisible in Google search."
8. **Barbershop 3:** "Your Instagram looks great, but Instagram doesn't rank on Google — that's lost walk-ins."
9. **Design Nails:** "13 years and 103 reviews, but no website — new customers can't find your price list or book."
10. **D'Vine Nails:** "170 reviews and growing, but only Instagram — a one-pager with a booking button captures the people who search."

**Why this works:** lead with the gap (no website = invisible on Google), make the outcome about *their* money (more bookings, found first), and end with one small ask (send an example), not "are you interested."
