# Outreach template (first message)

The rule: **personalized or nothing.** Every message references something specific about *their* business. Short, human, one clear ask. Never mass-blast.

Keep it to ~4 short lines. Goal of the first message is a reply, not a sale.

---

## Structure

1. **Specific observation** — something real about their business (a broken mobile site, a dead Instagram, hours wasted on a manual task).
2. **One-line value** — the outcome you'd create, in their terms.
3. **Soft proof** — one relevant past result or example (optional).
4. **One small ask** — a yes/no or a 15-min call. Never "let me know if interested."

---

## Example — Web

> Hi [Name], I was looking at [Business] and noticed the site is hard to use on a phone — which is where most of your customers probably find you. I build fast mobile-friendly sites for local businesses (recently did one for [X], who saw [result]). Worth a quick 15-min call this week to see if it's a fit?

## Example — Automation

> Hi [Name], saw that [Business] still takes bookings by phone and email. I set up systems that handle that automatically so you stop losing evenings to admin. Happy to show you what that'd look like for you — got 15 minutes Thursday?

## Example — Social

> Hi [Name], [Business]'s food looks great but the Instagram hasn't posted since [month]. I run social for local spots so the posting and replies just happen without you thinking about it. Want me to send a quick sample of what I'd do for you?

---

## Don't

- Don't open with "I hope this email finds you well."
- Don't list your services like a menu.
- Don't send the same message to two businesses. If you can swap the name and it still works, it's too generic — rewrite it.
