# Proposal template

Leads with **their problem and the outcome**, not your features. A client buys a result, not a website. Keep it one page where possible.

---

## [Client Name] — Proposal

**Prepared by:** Cal · **Date:** [date]

### 1. The problem
One or two plain sentences on what's costing them money, time, or customers right now. Use their words from the call.

### 2. What I'll do
The service, described as the outcome. Example: "A fast, mobile-first website that turns visitors into bookings." Then 3–5 bullet points of what's included.

- [Included item]
- [Included item]
- [Included item]

### 3. What's NOT included
Spell this out to prevent scope creep. Example: "Ongoing content updates, logo design, and paid ads are not included in this price."

### 4. Timeline
- Kickoff: [date]
- Draft / first version: [date]
- Launch: [date]

### 5. Price
**$[amount]** — [one line on terms, e.g. 50% to start, 50% on launch].

Optional: offer 2–3 tiers (Good / Better / Best) so the choice is "which one," not "yes or no."

### 6. Next step
One clear action. Example: "Reply 'yes' and I'll send the kickoff checklist and the deposit invoice."
