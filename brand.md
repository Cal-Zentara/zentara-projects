### Studio Setup

**What to borrow before recording anything:**
- Two LED panel lights (Neewer 660 or similar)
- A microphone — wireless clip-on like Holland Lark A1 is ideal, or any USB mic like Blue Yeti or Rode NT-USB Mini

**What to buy:**
- $16 Amazon Basics phone tripod (only purchase needed)

**Camera:** iPhone 16, rear camera only — never the front selfie cam

**iPhone settings before every recording:**
1. Open camera settings
2. Turn HDR **off**
3. Turn Lock White Balance **on**
4. Set resolution to **4K**
5. Set frame rate to **30fps**

**Background — TV Setup (65" TV):**
The background is an AI-generated image displayed on your TV. On camera it looks like a real studio. Nobody can tell it's a TV.

Vibe to generate: **warm and credible** — dark wood shelves, books, a warm lamp. Not tech-forward, not minimal white. Feels like a real person's space, not a developer's setup.

How to generate it: Open Claude, ask it to generate a studio background image. Prompt to use:
> "Generate a realistic YouTube studio background. Warm and credible feel. Dark wood bookshelf with books, a warm glowing lamp on one side, neutral wall. Soft warm lighting. Looks like a professional home office. No people, no screens visible, photorealistic."

Save the best version. Put it on the TV via screen share from your laptop or phone, or save to USB and plug into TV.

**TV setup order — follow this exactly:**
1. Generate and save background image on your laptop
2. Screen share or HDMI laptop to TV — image fills the full screen
3. Go into TV settings: turn off all sharpening, HDR, motion smoothing, fancy effects. Set to "movie" or "filmmaker" mode if available
4. Kill every light in the room — overhead, lamps, windows (use a blanket to block windows if needed). Total darkness.
5. Sit close to the TV — almost right up against it for a 65" TV
6. Place phone on tripod, dead center with the TV, perfectly level. No TV edges visible in frame.
7. Set up lights (next section)
8. Record a 10-second test clip. Watch it back. If the TV looks like a TV, adjust: sit closer, change camera angle, or tweak TV brightness settings.
9. If TV flickers in recording — set iPhone to exactly 30fps (flicker = frame rate mismatch)

**Lighting — follow this exactly:**
1. All room lights are already off (step 4 above)
2. Place light 1 (key light) at 45 degrees to your LEFT at face height — this is your brightest light, full power
3. Place light 2 (fill light) at 45 degrees to your RIGHT — set this to 1/3 the brightness of the key light
4. Both lights should be as close to your face as possible — closer = softer light
5. If you have a diffuser panel ($13 on Amazon) or a white bed sheet, put it in front of each light — this softens the shadows on your face
6. Match your lighting to the image on the TV — if the image has a lamp on the right side, make your fill light warmer to mimic it
7. Check: no harsh shadows on your face, no reflection on the TV screen, face is bright and clear

**Rule of thirds framing:**
- Your eyes should land on the top horizontal third of the frame
- Your head should be in the center column
- Leave a little headroom above your head — don't fill the frame with just your face

### Editing

**Tool: Descript** (descript.com)
Free plan to start. This is the only editing tool you need.

**How Descript works — it's not like normal editing:**
- Import your video files
- Descript transcribes everything you said automatically
- You edit by deleting words from the text — delete a sentence, it disappears from the video
- No scrubbing through footage, no timeline clicking

**Step by step editing process for every video:**
1. Go to descript.com, create free account
2. Create new project, import your video files (face cam + screen recording)
3. Let it transcribe (takes a few minutes)
4. Read through the transcript — delete every "um", "uh", long pause, or sentence that doesn't add anything
5. Turn on auto-captions — Descript styles them automatically
6. Change caption font to Poppins or Montserrat, white text, bold
7. Watch the video back — anywhere your face runs longer than 20 seconds, add a cut to the screen recording
8. Add zoom-ins on the screen when something important happens (Descript has a zoom tool)
9. Export as MP4

**Retention editing rules — do this on every video:**
- Cut every pause and filler word
- Something changes visually every 5–10 seconds (zoom, cut to screen, text appears)
- Never let face cam run longer than 20 seconds without cutting to screen recording
- Bold captions appear word-by-word as you speak
- Keep the first 15 seconds extremely tight — this is where people decide to stay or leave
